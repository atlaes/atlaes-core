GPR (DRV, Deutsche Rentenversicherung) Pension Refund Eligibility rules: 

Def: 
EU: European Union Member States
UK: United Kingdom
EEA: European Economic Area = Liechtenstein, Norway, Iceland


I) User type: Insured person, BEFORE German retirement age (Our main target group)

GENERAL RULES

1) Nationality
Non-EU/non-EEA/non-Swiss/non-UK
No dual citizenship with EU/EEA/Switzerland/UK allowed!

+

2) Residence
User must currently live in a non-EU/non-UK country
Living in the EEA or Switzerland is ok

+

3) Waiting Period
24 months must have passed since the last mandatory contribution (not voluntary) was paid.



ADDITIONAL RULES BY NATIONALITY: 
(Please note that this (except Non-Indians in India) to the residents of the mentioned countries. It only applies to the passport holder!)

USA, India, Australia, Canada, Brazil, Albania, Moldova, North Macedonia, The Philippines, South Korea, and Uruguay: A refund is only possible, if contribution period total < 60 months (maximum 59 months). 

Note: "1 month" is the smallest counting unit: If someone worked the last day in Jan and full month of Feb his pension account holds 2 months of contributions. 

Japanese: 
Japanese living in Japan: Same like above, 5 year max applies
(Japanese NOT living in Japan: no additional rules)

Former Yugoslav-State nationals living in any former Yugoslav state: No refund possible
(Former Yugoslav-State nationals NOT living in any former Yugoslav state: no additional rules)

Israeli nationals living in Israel: No refund
(Israeli nationals NOT living in Israel: no additional rules)

Turkish nationals living in Turkey: Also 24 months must have passed since the last mandatory pension contribution was paid in the TURKISH PENSION OFFICE. 
(For most Turkish in Turkey that means waiting until 2 years after retirement in Turkey which is earlier than German retirement before they can claim the refund)
(Turkish nationals living abroad: no extra rules)

Non-Indian Residents of India: All nationalities mentioned above can claim a refund. Every other nationality cannot claim a refund (treated like EU residents)


II) User type: Insured person, AFTER German retirement age

GENERAL RULES (only)

1) Contribution Period
A refund is only possible, if the total contribution period is max 59 months 
Else: Pension Claim

2) Nationality
A) EU including German/EEA/Swiss/UK and dual-passport holders
A refund is only possible, if the total contribution period combined EU/EEA/Swiss/UK is max 59 months 
Else: Interstate Pension Claim (Pension Claim)

B) Non-EU 
If 1) is met, ok!

III) User type: Widow of Insured person

GENERAL RULES

1) Contribution Period
A refund is only possible, if the insured person has not reached
a) 60 months in Germany (Else: Widow Pension) 
b) 60 months in Germany/EU/EEA/UK/Swiss combined (Else: Widow Pension) 


IV) User type: Orphan of Insured person

GENERAL RULES

1) Orphans can only claim a refund, if the insured has not left a spouse behind 
2) Several orphans split refund amount equally

2) Contribution Period
A refund is only possible, if the total contribution period is max 59 months 
Else: Orphan pension claim


For any user type what can affect the amount refunded: 

A) Benefits received
If the insured has received rehabilitation services (e.g. after a surgery, mental breakdown etc.), ALL contributions made prior to receiving those benefits are blocked and cannot be refunded. Only contributions made after receipt of benefits are refundable. 
(This can be very disappointing for users)

B) Pension received abroad under inclusion of German pension periods
If the user is receiving a pension outside of Germany which is paid under inclusion of German contribution periods, a refund is not possible. 

C) Pension adjustments from divorce settlements
The amount refunded can be increased/decreased by German court divorce settlements.
